## 📌 Overview

This project investigates the **flood retention efficiency** of the Thrissur North Kole wetlands — part of the internationally recognized **Vembanad–Kole Ramsar wetland system** in Kerala, India — using an integrated geospatial and hydrological modelling framework.

The Kole wetlands are one of the largest low-lying floodplain wetland agroecosystems in peninsular India, extending across approximately **13,632 hectares** between the Chalakkudy and Bharathapuzha Rivers. With elevations ranging from **0 to 1 m below mean sea level**, they are highly susceptible to seasonal inundation — yet simultaneously function as a critical natural flood buffer.

---

## 🎯 Research Objectives

- Independently replicate and extend a GIS-based digitization of the Thrissur North Kole wetlands at the *padasekharam* (bund-enclosed paddy unit) level
- Build a comprehensive **geospatial database** of wetland boundaries suitable for hydrological assessment
- Evaluate the **flood retention efficiency** of individual padasekharams during the **2018 Kerala floods**
- Develop a **flood susceptibility model** integrating topographic and hydrological variables
- Quantify the wetland's role in regional **flood mitigation and climate resilience**

The name *Kole* derives from a Malayalam word for a field capable of yielding a bumper crop — so long as flooding does not damage it. During the southwest monsoon, the landscape transforms into a **natural floodplain reservoir**, regulating regional hydrology and storing excess runoff.

## 🔬 Methodology

### 1. Wetland Digitization
- Replicated a published GIS-based digitization workflow for the Thrissur North Kole wetlands
- Collaborative spatial data development with boundary refinement and validation
- Output: padasekharam-level spatial inventory of wetland polygons

### 2. Hydrological Modelling (ArcGIS Pro)
- Digital Elevation Model (DEM) processing
- Flow direction and flow accumulation modelling
- Watershed delineation and slope analysis
- Integration of historical flood extent data (2018 Kerala floods)

### 3. Flood Retention Analysis
- Spatial overlay of historical flood extents with wetland polygons
- **Surface-volume analysis** to estimate floodwater retention capacity per padasekharam
- Assessment of inundation dynamics and flood pathways

### 4. Flood Susceptibility Modelling
- Integration of topographic and hydrological variables
- Evaluation of flood dynamics under existing wetland conditions

## ⚠️ Context & Threats

Despite their ecological and economic importance, the Kole wetlands face mounting pressures:

- Rapid **urbanization** and land-use transformation
- **Wetland reclamation** for agriculture and development
- **Climate-induced extreme rainfall** events (as seen in the 2018 Kerala floods)
- Limited availability of high-resolution spatial datasets at the padasekharam scale
